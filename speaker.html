Peeple Sound System: building DIY speakers for warm analog nights

This system was made by hand for the room. From raw wood sheets to finished cabinets, Peeple Sound System built a setup that feels alive: soft on top, deep in the lows, and warm in the middle where voices and melodies live.

The core builder is Master P, a local Thai craftsman and one of the Muang Ek Soundsystem members. He is also a DJ, playing reggae, dub, techno, and house, often with vinyl in his sets.

01 / Start from materials

The first phase looked more like a garage table than a club stage. Panels were measured, cut, and checked again. Every angle mattered because small errors later become unwanted resonance.

02 / Cabinet, horn, and tuning

After joining and bracing the boxes, the team tuned the cabinet response by ear and by feel. Horn angles, damping choices, and crossover balance were adjusted until transients hit clean but never harsh.

Master P approached the tuning like a selector and a DJ at once: keeping enough pressure for dance music, while preserving the round, musical tone needed for reggae and dub bass lines.

03 / Warm analog character in the room

The final voice of the system is intentionally warm and analog. Kick drums keep body, bass lines stay round, and highs remain present without becoming sharp. At Wasabi House, this gives long sets a human, breathing tone.
